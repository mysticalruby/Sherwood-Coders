Have you heard of the number π? What about i? Well, learn about these interesting numbers in this page!

Now, you may have probably heard of the numer π. However, this number is mor mathematically interesting than 3.14! π is a number defined to multiply by the radius in a circle to find its area or circumference. The area of somnething is how much space is inside it, and the curcumference of a circle is the outer length of the shape. To find the area of a circle, you use the formula πrr, where r is the radius. a radius is the halfway length of circle. If the radius of a circle is 5, for example, we would sqaure 5 to get 25. Lastly we multiply π by 25 to get 25π, which is the answer! We typically don't get accurate representations of pi. ∏ is capital pi and π is lowercase pi. ∏ is an irrational number, meaning that the digits terminate, or never end. ∏ is approximately 22/7, which equals to 3.1428, while π is actually 3.141592. Because π is an irrational number, it cannot be accrately represented as a fraction. Many famous mathematicians have created patterns to find π, and one common example is includes the presence of odd numbers. It is: 1/1-1/3+1/5-1/7+1/9-1/11... = π.

Okay- now lets learn about intervals and i. What are intervals? Interval notation is the notation used to write the answer to an inequality. An inequality is basically an equation, which includes finding a variable value, besides there is no equal symbol. Instead, there is a lesser than, greater than, or an equals to plus them. For eample, in the inequality 3+x is lesser than 15, we know x is not greater or equal to 12, because that equals to 15 itself, and anything beyond that will result in a sum being greater than 15. So, we can narrow down our answer to being less than 12. So, x is less than 12! However, so many answers could be x, and we cannot possibly find the ONLY answer because there isn't only one. So, including tghe decimals and other numbers, there are infinitely many answes to replace for x. How do we represent all these infinite answers in a finite expression? That is where interval notation comes in!

Interval notation repreents an infinite number of answers through -infinity and +infinity.
